Introduction

"We don't bet on ideas. We invest in the infrastructure that makes AI possible: a trillion-dollar growing market, where Base A.I acts as a national pioneer in AI infrastructure as a real asset."

Base A.I Mission

Computational power has become the most valuable asset of the 21st century: the resource that sustains all innovations in Artificial Intelligence.

Our mission is to democratize financial access to AI infrastructure, transforming high-performance servers and GPUs into real, traceable, and profitable investment assets.

Base A.I structures private infrastructure participation frameworks that allow any investor to participate in the global AI economy through data centers and GPU clusters with contracted demand.

We offer direct exposure to revenue generated by companies using our computing resources, with transparency, governance, and predictability.

Our goal is to be the leading platform for AI infrastructure financing and management, enabling private shareholders to benefit from the growth of this new physical-digital asset market.

As the sector evolves, we will expand our solutions to include new areas of advanced computing, such as edge computing, which brings data processing closer to the user, reducing costs and response time; quantum computing, the next frontier of performance in complex calculations; and distributed inference, which allows multiple machines to cooperate to execute AI models faster and more efficiently.

Solution Overview

AI Infrastructure as a Real Asset

Data Centers, GPUs and Monthly Revenue Share

Base A.I connects private shareholders to real computing infrastructures, such as high-performance data centers and corporate GPUs (NVIDIA, AMD, Dell, Supermicro).

Each funding round is dedicated to the acquisition and operation of clusters that are leased to companies in the AI sector: from generative modeling startups to large corporate groups that demand computational power at scale, such as Amazon, Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Stability AI, Hugging Face, NVIDIA Cloud, Oracle Cloud, Databricks, and Snowflake.

Private shareholders receive proportional participation in the operational revenue generated by the assets monthly, proportional to their stake in the project.

The entire process is audited, with real-time performance dashboard, utilization metrics, uptime, and billing.

The structure is based on private contracts in Brazil, ensuring legal compliance, legal security, and predictable revenue flow, without public issuance of securities.
