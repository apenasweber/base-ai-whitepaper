Data Center Partnership for Infrastructure Investment and GPU Allocation

Overview

At Base AI Capital, we invest in data center infrastructure and GPU allocation to generate shared revenue with operational partners. Our focus is to build or expand data centers, install high-performance clusters (GPUs), and establish usage contracts with companies, cloud providers, or R&D centers.

How It Works

  1. We identify or establish partnerships with data centers looking to expand and/or upgrade their infrastructure.
  2. We conduct fundraising rounds of different sizes with varied objectives:
    • Investment via GPUs in existing data centers;
    • Investment in new data centers for companies that already own facilities and want expansion (new branches or regions).
  3. We provide GPU clusters for deployment in the chosen data center infrastructure.
  4. Profits from operations (GPU usage) are shared between the partner data center and Base AI Capital, as per agreement.
  5. We establish transparency, governance, and infrastructure performance reports to ensure alignment among all parties.

Market Opportunity

The global GPU market for data centers is estimated at $119.97 billion in 2025 and could reach approximately $228.04 billion by 2030, with a CAGR of approximately 13.7%. Source: Markets and Markets

Other estimates point to growth of about 14% between 2025 and 2030, reaching $197.55 billion. Source: Deep Market Insights

Benefits for Partner Data Centers

  • Monetization of idle or underutilized infrastructure on a recurring basis.
  • Revenue participation with a revenue share model, with clear profit division.
  • Infrastructure expansion or upgrade without the need to assume all capital.
  • Partnership with a manager specialized in AI and infrastructure, handling all GPU allocation, contracts, and operations.

Fundraising Models and Scope

  • Small-scale investments for initial entry with GPU clusters (GPU pod or dedicated rack) in existing data centers.
  • Medium or large-scale fundraising for building new data center regions.
  • Capacity expansion for companies that already own data centers and want to add AI/ML infrastructure.
  • Flexible models: dedicated GPUs (single client) or shared pool.
